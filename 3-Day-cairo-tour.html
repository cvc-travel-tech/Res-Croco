Day 1, Arrival at Cairo, Optional Sound and Light Show (Wednesday)
  • Arrive at Cairo International Airport and you will find our representative waiting for you after receiving your luggage & clearing customs. You, then, will reach the hotel and the rest of the day will be free at your leisure.

  • In the evening, you may choose our Optional Sound and Light Show in front of the three pyramids of Giza plateau. Illuminated against the night sky, historical narrative and impressive show will be presented guaranteeing unforgettable night.
    • Day 2, Cairo City Tour (Thursday)
  • Our day starts with a visit to the one and only standing monument among the seven wonders of the ancient world, the Great Pyramid of Khufu. In front of the glorious building, you will know and see why the Great Pyramid was chosen to be the most famous monument in the whole world. Carrying on to Giza plateau, you will see The Pyramids of Khafra, Menkaura, the well-preserved valley temple of Khafra and the Great Sphinx guarding the area

  • Continue to the Egyptian Museum in El-Tahrir Square, the home of Egypt's most valuable antiquities. You will be amazed by the number of treasures gathered in this building. It is highly recommended that you access "the Royal Mummy Room", at extra fees, to have a glimpse of the mummified great kings and queens of Ancient Egypt!

  • After having lunch, we proceed to discover Cairo by visiting the area of Old Cairo, also known as the "Center of Religions". The area encompasses many Coptic churches. There are also the synagogue of Ben Ezra and the Mosque of Amr Ibn Al-Aas very near to the complex.

  • Meals served: Breakfast and Lunch.
    • Day 3, Travel to Aswan, Nile Cruise Sightseeing (Friday)
  • Early morning, you will arrive at Cairo International Airport to catch the flight to Aswan
  • Upon arrival, you will then reach the luxuries Nile Cruise, where you will spend the next three nights, to start your relaxing tour sailing from Aswan to Luxor.
  • After having lunch, the Nile Cruise excursion will start by visiting The Aswan High Dam built across the Nile between 1960 and 1970. The importance of The High Dam lies in controlling the annual flood, providing increased storage of water used for irrigation and generating hydroelectricity. It has significant influence on the Egyptian economy and culture.

  • The last stop of the day will be Philae Temple. The whole temple was dismantled and reassembled within the UNESO project to save it from a watery grave during the construction of Aswan High Dam. The ancient temple was dedicated to the fertility goddess Isis and it has artifacts, scenes and statues from different history layers

  • At night you may choose the Optional breathtaking Sound and Light show at Philae Temple

  • Meals served: Lunch and Dinner.
